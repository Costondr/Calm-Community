A number your doctor watches closely. A number you've probably obsessed over. A number that can make you feel like a success or a failure in the span of a single lab result.

But here's what your A1C doesn't show — and why that matters more than you think.

## What A1C Actually Measures

Your A1C reflects your *average* blood glucose over roughly three months. Think of it like the average temperature in your city for the season — it tells you something, but it doesn't tell you about the day it hit 98°F or the morning you needed a jacket in July.

Two people can have identical A1C values and completely different daily experiences. One might run steady, smooth, predictable lines. The other might spend half their time low and half their time high — averaging out to the same number.

## The Gap Between the Number and the Life

Here's what A1C can't capture:

- The 3am alarm that woke you — again
- The meeting you had to leave because you were dropping fast
- The meal you calculated perfectly and still spiked to 280
- The anxiety that lives in the back of your mind every single day

None of that shows up in your lab results. But all of it is real. All of it is part of living with T1D.

## What We Should Be Measuring Instead

Time in Range (TIR) — the percentage of time your glucose stays between 70 and 180 mg/dL — tells a richer story. So does time spent low. So does glucose variability.

But even those numbers don't capture quality of life, mental health, the cognitive load of constant calculation, or the emotional weight of a disease that never takes a day off.

> "The goal isn't a perfect number. The goal is a full life."

## The CALM Perspective

At CALM, we believe your A1C is *one data point* — not a verdict on your worth, your effort, or your future.

You are managing something extraordinarily complex, every single day, with imperfect tools and imperfect information. The fact that you're still here, still trying, still adjusting — that's the whole story. And no lab result can measure that.
